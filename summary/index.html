Welcome to Surnumerism

Welcome to Surnumerism, an open-source book on a labor-based economy.

Surnumerism highlights what is most precious about humans: Humans are active participants in their environment. They work. Others endure their environment and adapt to it. Humans act upon their environment, transforming it through their knowledge and effort.

The Value of Work

The difference between humans and animals is not only reflection, but also usufruct: work. Possession is of little importance. Producing, reproducing, and then innovating is what matters. It is work that gives value, and it must be the determining criterion between humans. Work must be sought after, valued, and remunerated.

A Call to Action

Is man's son ready to share the work and not just make others work? In ancient times, there was slavery. Nowadays, wages are a form of exploitation of man by his fellow man.

We challenge the foremost men and nations of so-called civilized societies to rise above the masses still enslaved by a medieval mentality.

Surnumerism is like the proverb that masterfully exhorts us to learn to fish rather than give fish to the needy. No new truth will emerge from Surnumerism, but it is time for man to wise up and realize that his best ally is none other than the human within himself. No entity, no machine, and no wealth can assist him as much as man himself in his journey toward full, balanced, and shared development.

About the Author

Wilfried St Dial is a pseudonym that reflects the date (October 12, 1492) of the discovery of the New World: a symbol for a new quest across the world. This date is also the author's birthday. His name is an English evocation of the dial of humanity and a statement of his surname.
