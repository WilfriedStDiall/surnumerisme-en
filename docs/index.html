Introduction

Philosophical Concept

The bubble is shared and vital

The planet has become so fragile and small that it can be likened to a soap bubble threatening to burst at any moment. Our future is shared and uncertain, as we are threatened by natural (climate change), artificial (pollution), economic, and social (intolerable poverty) dangers.

The solution that will save us must be acceptable to all and for all. No more half-measures. How can we accept that nearly 60% of the world's population will soon be without drinking water? And yet, wealth and technological innovations are out of step with the social situation. The bubble must crystallize! (Reference: UN Report 2023, 2.2 billion affected)

Presentation

We know we may be guilty of utopianism, but we would so much like the rich to become ever richer while creating new rich people.

The name Surnumerism is new to dictionaries and encyclopedias, and so is its concept, although it is nothing revolutionary: it is a synergy of ancient concepts. A receptive state of mind. We dare to hope that everyone, from the most uneducated among us to the most powerful in the world, will have an attentive conscience and a proactive metamorphosis for a more viable common future.

Analysis of the global situation

At the dawn of this new millennium, it is clear that humanity suffers from countless social, ideological, and religious disparities, the Gordian knot of which is the economy.

As long as inequalities continue to grow between North and South, between citizens of the same country, and ultimately between the rich and the poor, we will continue to experience tensions that are essentially economic in nature, regardless of the form (immigration, ideology, religion, unionism, armed rebellion, etc.) in which individuals express their despair.

Soon, the world will become unliveable even for the rich, who will be forced to shut themselves away, and the decline of this wonderful civilization seems inevitable.

And yet, scientific and technological progress, unparalleled in human history, promised something completely different. Human resources, means of production, and natural resources are virtually unlimited, but humanity finds itself trapped by human egocentrism, because sharing goods and knowledge is not natural to the human race.

Human Mind

As stated, our civilization has sufficient human and productive resources to satisfy all material needs. However, we are threatened by inadequacies (impoverishment) and societal unrest.
