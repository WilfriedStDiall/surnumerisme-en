Chapter 8: Global Applicability – Towards a Global Hybrid Model

Introduction: An Overview

Surnumerism is spreading via a global network, using currency as its universal foundation. The African Union plays a key role in coordinating regional adoption across the continent.

Projections

  • 15% reduction in the Gini coefficient over 20 years.
  • Fivefold increase in SMEs through monetary confidence, with a projected 3-fold increase in sub-Saharan Africa by 2045.

Institutional Roles

  • UN: Coordination via ECOSOC.
  • IMF: Initial loans, strengthening the currency as legal tender.
  • AfDB (African Development Bank): Supports infrastructure financing in Africa.

Conclusion

Surnumerism unifies nations, honoring currency. The following chapter responds to critics, addressing challenges such as infrastructure gaps in Africa.
